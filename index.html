What is Computational Thinking?

Computational Thinking is a structured problem-solving process that enables individuals to tackle complex challenges by breaking them into smaller components, identifying patterns, focusing on important information, designing logical solutions, implementing them effectively, and continuously evaluating results for improvement.

Computational Thinking Cycle

Understand

Define the challenge clearly, gather information, identify requirements, and understand the desired outcome.

Decompose

Break a complex problem into smaller manageable parts that can be solved individually.

Design

Create logical strategies, workflows, algorithms and plans for solving the challenge.

Implement

Apply the designed solution using technology, models, systems or practical methods.

Evaluate

Test results, identify weaknesses, optimize performance and improve outcomes.
